How to Set Up Your NoBorder VPN: A Comprehensive Guide

Welcome to NoBorder VPN, your gateway to a secure and unrestricted internet experience! This guide will walk you through the process of setting up your VPN connection on various devices and platforms. Whether you're on the go with your smartphone or working from your desktop, we'll help you get connected quickly and easily. While we focus on NoBorder VPN, the principles apply broadly to many VPN services.

Understanding VPN Protocols and Apps

Before diving into setup, it's helpful to understand a few key terms:

  • VPN Protocol: This is the set of rules that defines how your VPN connection is established and secured. Common protocols include VLESS, Shadowsocks, OpenVPN, WireGuard, and IKEv2. NoBorder VPN leverages advanced protocols like VLESS and Shadowsocks for optimal performance and evasion.
  • VPN Client/App: This is the software you install on your device to connect to the VPN server.
  • Configuration File/Link: This contains all the necessary server details (address, port, protocol, encryption, etc.) for your VPN client to connect.

Setting Up Your VPN on Android

Android users have excellent options for connecting to NoBorder VPN servers. We'll cover Hiddify and v2rayNG.

1. Setup with Hiddify (Recommended for VLESS/Shadowsocks)

Hiddify is a powerful and user-friendly client that supports multiple protocols, including VLESS and Shadowsocks, making it ideal for NoBorder VPN users.

  1. Download Hiddify:
    • Open the Google Play Store on your Android device.
    • Search for "Hiddify" (or "Hiddify Next").
    • Tap "Install" and wait for the app to download.
  2. Obtain Your Configuration:
    • Log in to your NoBorder VPN account on our website.
    • Navigate to your subscription details or server information page.
    • Look for a "Subscription Link" or "QR Code". Copy the link or prepare to scan the QR code.
  3. Import Configuration into Hiddify:
    • Open the Hiddify app.
    • Tap the "+" icon (usually in the bottom right or top right corner).
    • Choose "Add from Clipboard" if you copied the subscription link, or "Scan QR Code" if you have the QR code displayed on another screen.
    • Hiddify will automatically import all server configurations.
  4. Connect to NoBorder VPN:
    • Once imported, you'll see a list of available servers.
    • Select your desired server (e.g., based on location or latency).
    • Tap the large "Connect" button (often a power icon or a switch).
    • The first time you connect, Android will ask for permission to set up a VPN connection. Tap "OK" or "Allow".
    • The connection status will change to "Connected" or "Active" when successful.

2. Setup with v2rayNG (Alternative for VLESS/Shadowsocks)

v2rayNG is another robust client widely used for VLESS and Shadowsocks protocols.

  1. Download v2rayNG:
    • Open the Google Play Store.
    • Search for "v2rayNG".
    • Tap "Install".
  2. Obtain Your Configuration:
    • Log in to your NoBorder VPN account.
    • Copy your "VLESS" or "Shadowsocks" subscription link or individual server configuration.
  3. Import Configuration into v2rayNG:
    • Open the v2rayNG app.
    • Tap the "+" icon in the top right corner.
    • Choose "Import config from clipboard" if you copied a single server configuration.
    • If you have a subscription link, choose "Import config from clipboard" then tap the three dots in the top right and select "Update subscription" (or similar option).
  4. Connect to NoBorder VPN:
    • Once imported, you'll see your server configurations.
    • Tap the round button (often a "V" icon) at the bottom right to connect.
    • Grant VPN connection permission when prompted.
    • The button will turn green, and a key icon will appear in your notification bar when connected.

Setting Up Your VPN on Windows, macOS, and Linux

For desktop operating systems, we recommend the official V2RayN (Windows), V2RayX/Qv2ray (macOS/Linux) or a general Shadowsocks client.

1. Setup with V2RayN (Windows)

V2RayN is a popular choice for VLESS and Shadowsocks on Windows.

  1. Download V2RayN:
    • Go to the V2RayN GitHub Releases page.
    • Download the latest `v2rayN-Core.zip` file.
    • Extract the contents to a folder on your computer (e.g., `C:\V2RayN`).
  2. Obtain Your Configuration:
    • Log in to your NoBorder VPN account.
    • Copy your "Subscription Link" (preferably VLESS or Shadowsocks).
  3. Import Configuration into V2RayN:
    • Run `v2rayN.exe`. It might appear in your system tray (bottom right).
    • Right-click the V2RayN icon in the system tray.
    • Select "Subscriptions" > "Subscription settings...".
    • Click "Add", paste your subscription link into the "Address(Url)" field, give it a remark (e.g., "NoBorder VPN"), and click "OK".
    • Right-click the V2RayN icon again, select "Subscriptions" > "Update subscription". This will fetch your server list.
  4. Connect to NoBorder VPN:
    • Right-click the V2RayN icon.
    • Go to "Servers" and select your desired NoBorder VPN server.
    • Right-click the icon again and choose "System Proxy" > "Set System Proxy (Auto Mode)".
    • A green icon or "Connected" status indicates success.

2. Setup with Qv2ray (macOS/Linux)

Qv2ray is a cross-platform GUI client for VLESS and other protocols.

  1. Download Qv2ray:
    • Go to the Qv2ray GitHub Releases page.
    • Download the appropriate version for your OS (e.g., `.dmg` for macOS, `.AppImage` or `deb`/`rpm` for Linux).
    • Install or run the application.
  2. Install V2Ray Core:
    • Qv2ray requires the V2Ray core. In Qv2ray, go to "Preferences" > "Kernel Settings".
    • Click "Download V2Ray Core".
  3. Obtain Your Configuration:
    • Log in to your NoBorder VPN account and copy your "Subscription Link".
  4. Import Configuration into Qv2ray:
    • In Qv2ray, go to "Groups" > "New Subscription".
    • Paste your subscription link and click "OK".
    • Right-click on the newly added subscription group and select "Update Subscription".
  5. Connect to NoBorder VPN:
    • Select your desired NoBorder VPN server from the list.
    • Click the "Connect" button (often a plug icon).
    • Ensure "System Proxy" is enabled in the Qv2ray settings for system-wide VPN.

3. Generic Shadowsocks Client (Windows/macOS/Linux)

If you prefer a dedicated Shadowsocks client, these are widely available.

  1. Download Shadowsocks Client:
    • For Windows: Shadowsocks-Windows.
    • For macOS: ShadowsocksX-NG.
    • For Linux: `shadowsocks-qt5` or command-line clients.
    • Install the client.
  2. Obtain Your Shadowsocks Configuration:
    • Log in to your NoBorder VPN account.
    • Copy your "Shadowsocks" configuration link (ss://...) or individual server details (server address, port, password, encryption method).
  3. Import Configuration:
    • Open the Shadowsocks client.
    • Usually, you can click a "+" or "Add Profile" button.
    • Choose to import from a URL (paste the ss:// link) or manually enter the server details.
  4. Connect:
    • Select the imported server.
    • Click "Connect" or toggle the switch to enable.
    • Ensure the system proxy settings in the Shadowsocks client are configured to direct traffic through the VPN.

Troubleshooting Common Setup Issues

Even with clear instructions, issues can arise. Here are some common problems and their solutions:

  • "Connection Failed" / "Timeout":
    • Check Internet Connection: Ensure your regular internet connection is working without the VPN.
    • Try Another Server: Your chosen server might be overloaded or temporarily down. Switch to another server from your NoBorder VPN list.
    • Check Protocol: Ensure your client is using the correct protocol (VLESS, Shadowsocks, etc.) as specified in your configuration.
    • Firewall/Antivirus: Temporarily disable your firewall or antivirus to see if it's blocking the VPN connection. If it works, add an exception for your VPN client.
    • System Time: Ensure your device's system time is accurate and synchronized. Incorrect time can cause certificate validation failures.
  • "No Internet After Connecting":
    • DNS Issues: Your VPN client might not be correctly setting DNS. Check if your client has an option to use custom DNS (e.g., 1.1.1.1 or 8.8.8.8).
    • Kill Switch: If your client has a "Kill Switch" feature, ensure it's not preventing internet access when the VPN is off or disconnected.
    • Proxy Settings: On desktop, check your browser and system proxy settings. They should usually be set to "Auto-detect" or "No proxy" unless specifically configured otherwise by the VPN client.
  • Configuration Import Errors:
    • Corrupted Link/QR: Ensure you copied the entire link or scanned the QR code completely and clearly. Try re-copying/re-scanning.
    • Unsupported Format: Some clients only support specific protocols or formats. Double-check that the configuration you're importing matches what your app expects (e.g., `ss://` for Shadowsocks, a full subscription link for Hiddify).
